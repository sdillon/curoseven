Our Story

CuroSeven is the brainchild of two medical doctors who are both athletes.

At CuroSeven we take our knowledge as doctors and athletes to help you achieve your optimal performance potential and take a preventative approach to future disease through early identification.

We believe that our mental state is the key to unlocking that extra potential within us all. To give us the edge over our competition. When everyone is putting 100% into what they are doing then it's what is going on inside your head that makes the difference between being on the podium, getting your PB and not. We have a huge passion in helping people to understand themselves better and to use that understanding to make them perform better or recover better or focus better. We think that understanding how your body works physically and mentally and monitoring key elements can help you achieve that goal that has seemed impossible.

Background

Dr Tamsin Lewis is a one of the top ranked Ironman 70.3 athletes in the world and races as a professional triathlete. Tamsin studied Medicine & Surgery at Kings College London and also has a Bachelor of Science degree in NeuroScience and Anatomy.

Post graduation she worked as hospital doctor in Medicine and Anaesthetics for two years before pursuing a post-graduate Royal College of Psychiatrists qualification and a career in psychiatry.

Training as a triathlete alongside working full-time as a doctor, Tamsin won the World Age-Group Olympic Distance Triathlon Championships in 2009 and was offered the opportunity by Brett Sutton to join Team TBB and ‘turn pro’. Since 2010 Tamsin has raced and podiumed around the world in triathlon.

During this time of racing and training as a professional athlete, Tamsin has experienced first hand, injury, overtraining, anaemia, mineral depletion and misdiagnosis by NHS services.

As a result this fuelled a desire to help others overcome what she learnt the hard way. Studying for a Masters in Sports Medicine afforded the opportunity to research what is required to perform at your best. In addition Tamsin has worked with some of the top triathlon coaches in the world and has an ever inquisitive mind to keep learning and improving.

2013 was Tamsins most successful racing season. She attributes this to close management of a variety of blood markers to monitor fatigue and recovery in conjunction with a strong coaching relationship.

Background

Dr Claire Gillvray is a GP with a special interest in sports medicine and mental health. She has competed in triathlon at a high level as an age grouper.Claire was born and brought up in Northern Ireland. Like Tamsin, she swam competitively until the age of 15 which instilled a level of fitness and discipline.

Claire studied Medicine and Surgery at Edinburgh University. Post qualification Claire worked in various specialties – Emergency Medicine, Paediatrics & Geriatrics before settling into Psychiatry and completed membership of The Royal College of Psychiatrists in 2004. Claire’s sub-speciality in Psychiatry was Addictions and Psychotherapy.

In 2006, Claire retrained as a General Practioner, completing MRCGP and in 2011 completed a Master in Sports Medicine at Bath University. The dissertation piece compared the Addictive Traits of Triathletes, with those of Alcoholics.

Claire is a highly accomplished triathlete and has a sub 11 hour Ironman time in addition to a number of international Age-Group wins. She has the very realistic aim to place top 20 in The World Ironman Championships, held in Kona, Hawaii.
